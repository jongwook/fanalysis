Consider a series of function $$f_n : [0, 1] \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$$ where $$ n = 1, 2, 3, \cdots$$, defined as

$$f_n(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & \mathrm{if} ~ x < \frac {n-1} {2n} \\ nx - \frac {n-1} 2 & \mathrm{if} ~ x \in \left [ \frac {n-1} {2n} , \frac {n+1} {2n}\right ] \\ 1 & \mathrm{if} ~ x > \frac {n+1} {2n} \end{cases}$$

The $$f_n(x)$$ is continuous on $$[0, 1]$$, i.e.  $$f_n \in C([0, 1])$$ for all $$n = 1, 2, 3, \cdots $$, since

$$ f_n (\frac {n-1} {2n}) = n \cdot \frac {n-1} {2n} - \frac {n-1} 2 = \frac {n-1} 2 - \frac {n-1} 2 = 0 = \lim_{x \rightarrow {\frac {n-1} {2n}}^{+} } f_n (\frac {n-1} {2n})$$ and 
$$ f_n (\frac {n+1} {2n}) = n \cdot \frac {n+1} {2n} - \frac {n-1} 2 = \frac {n+1} 2 - \frac {n-1} 2 = 0 = \lim_{x \rightarrow {\frac {n+1} {2n}}^{-} } f_n (\frac {n+1} {2n})$$.

$$(f_n)$$ is a Cauchy sequence, since for given $$\epsilon < 0$$, take $$N = \left \lceil \frac 2 {\epsilon} \right \rceil $$, then for all $$n > m > N$$,

$$ \left \| ~f_n - f_m~ \right \|_{L^{1}} = \int_0^{\frac {N-1} {2N}} | f_n(x) - f_m(x) | dx + \int_{\frac {N-1} {2N}}^{\frac {N+1} {2N}} | f_n(x) - f_m(x) | dx + \int_{\frac {N+1} {2N}}^1 | f_n(x) - f_m(x) | dx  $$
$$ \leq \int_0^{\frac {N-1} {2N}} | 0-0 | dx + \int_{\frac {N-1} {2N}}^{\frac {N+1} {2N}} | f_n(x) | dx + \int_{\frac {N-1} {2N}}^{\frac {N+1} {2N}} | f_m(x)| dx + \int_{\frac {N+1} {2N}}^1 | 0-0 | dx$$
$$ \leq \int_{\frac {N-1} {2N}}^{\frac {N+1} {2N}} dx + \int_{\frac {N-1} {2N}}^{\frac {N+1} {2N}} dx ~ ( \because \forall n \in \mathbb{N} : f_n \leq 1)$$
$$ = \frac 1 N + \frac 1 N = \frac 2 N < \epsilon $$

However, as we take $$ n \rightarrow \infty$$, $$ f_n$$ converges to $$  f(x) = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} f_n(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & \mathrm{if} ~ x < \frac {1} {2} \\ \frac 1 2 & \mathrm{if} ~ x = \frac 1 2 \\ 1 & \mathrm{if} ~ x > \frac {1} {2} \end{cases}  $$

which is not continuous, i.e. f is not in C([0, 1]). Therefore C([0, 1]) is not complete, thus not a Banach space.
