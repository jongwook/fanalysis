$$H_0^\perp$$ is an orthogonal complement and this it is closed.

For any Cauchy sequence $$(a_n + b_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$$ in $$H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp $$ where $$ a_n \in H_0 $$ and $$ b_n \in H_0^\perp $$ and any $$n, m \in \mathbb{N}$$, 

$$ \left < (a_n + b_n) - (a_m + b_m) , (a_n + b_n) - (a_m + b_m) \right > \\
= \left < (a_n - a_m) + (b_n - b_m) , (a_n - a_m) + (b_n - b_m) \right > \\
= \left < a_n - a_m , a_n - a_m \right > + 2 \left < a_n - a_m , b_n - b_m \right > + \left < b_n - b_m , b_n - b_m \right > 
= \left < a_n - a_m , a_n - a_m \right > + \left < b_n - b_m , b_n - b_m \right > \\ 
$$

, where the last equality comes from the orthogonality of $$H_0$$ and $$H_0^\perp$$. This implies that $$a_n$$ and $$b_n$$ are Cauchy sequences in $$H_0$$ and $$H_0^\perp$$ and converge to some $$ a \in H_0 $$ and $$ b \in H_0^\perp $$, whence $$a_n + b_n$$ converges to $$ a + b \in H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp $$. Therefore we have that $$H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp$$ is closed.

If $$H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp \neq H $$, there exists a nonzero $$x \in H - (H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp)$$ from which we can take its orthogonal projection to $$H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp$$ whose difference between $$x$$ is orthogonal to to $$H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp \neq H $$: There exists some nonzero $$ x_0 \in H $$ such that $$x_0$$ is orthogonal to $$H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp \neq H $$.

Now $$x_0$$ is orthogonal to both $$H_0$$ and $$H_0^\perp$$, which means $$x_0 \in H_0^\perp \cap H_0^{\perp\perp} = H_0^\perp \oplus H_0 = \left \{ 0 \right \} $$ and $$ x = 0 $$ and this contradicts that $$ x $$ is nonzero. 

Therefore we conclude that $$ H = H_0 \oplus H_0^\perp $$.
