Suppose a step function in $$L^\infty ([0,1])$$ whose step is at $$ \frac 1 2 $$.

$$f(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & \mathrm{if} ~ n \leq \frac 1 2 \\ 1 & \mathrm{if} ~ n > \frac 1 2 \end{cases}$$

If $$\mathcal{D}$$ is dense, there exists a smooth (thus continuous) function $$g$$ such that $$|| f - g ||_{L^\infty} < \frac 1 3$$ .

From continuity we have the left limit $$ \lim_{x \rightarrow {\frac 1 2}^{-} } g(x) < \frac 1 3$$, since otherwise $$|| f - g ||_{L^\infty}$$ becomes larger than $$\frac 1 3$$,

similarly we have the right limit $$ \lim_{x \rightarrow {\frac 1 2}^{+} } g(x) > \frac 2 3$$, since otherwise $$|| f - g ||_{L^\infty}$$ becomes larger than $$\frac 1 3$$.

So this implies $$  \lim_{x \rightarrow {\frac 1 2}^{-} } g(x) \neq  \lim_{x \rightarrow {\frac 1 2}^{+} } g(x)$$ which contradicts that $$g$$ is continuous.

Therefore $$\mathcal{D}$$ is not dense in $$L^\infty$$.
